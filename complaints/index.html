§1 Our Commitment

AI Lane Limited is committed to handling all complaints professionally, fairly, and transparently. We treat every complaint as a closed-loop operational event: it is acknowledged promptly, evaluated on its merits against the evidence, resolved within defined parameters, and closed with a complete record.

We will always communicate with you in plain language. We will not dismiss your complaint without giving you a reasoned response. Where we have made an error, we will acknowledge it and take appropriate action.

§2 How to Raise a Complaint

You can raise a complaint by any of the following methods:

  • Email: [email protected]
  • Post: AI Lane Limited

Please include the following information where possible:

  • The date of your purchase or the date of the issue.
  • Your Stripe payment reference (found in your payment confirmation email).
  • A clear description of your complaint or the issue you have experienced.
  • What outcome you are seeking (refund, correction, explanation, or other).
  • Any supporting evidence you wish us to consider.

You are not required to provide all of this information to raise a complaint, but it will help us to assess your case more efficiently.

§3 What We Can Help With

We can accept and assess complaints relating to:

  • Technical failures that prevented you from receiving your Assessment Output.
  • Assessment Outputs that you believe contain a material error in the application of statutory requirements.
  • Billing errors, duplicate charges, or payment processing issues.
  • Refund requests on grounds set out in our Terms of Service §7.
  • Our conduct or communications in connection with the Service.
  • Data protection and privacy concerns (handled under a separate process — see §8).

Outside scope. We are unable to accept complaints on the grounds that an Assessment Output did not produce a finding you expected, or that the outcome of a real employment dispute differed from what an Assessment Output suggested. Assessment Outputs are analytical tools, not legal advice, and we cannot accept liability for decisions made on their basis without independent professional advice.

§4 The ADRA Process

AI Lane Limited operates an AI Dispute Resolution Agent (ADRA) as the primary evaluation mechanism for complaints and refund requests. The ADRA operates under the direct oversight of the CEO and within defined authority parameters.

4.1 How ADRA evaluates your complaint

  1. Your complaint is received and acknowledged within 2 business days. An initial classification is applied (technical failure, refund request, conduct, data, or chargeback dispute).
  2. ADRA retrieves the relevant Scan Record, payment record, and any previous communications from the platform audit trail.
  3. ADRA assesses your complaint against the evidence on record, the Terms of Service, and this Complaint Handling Policy. It applies a structured evaluation framework.
  4. ADRA produces a reasoned written decision: Approve, Partial Approve, or Decline — with a clear explanation of its reasoning referenced to the applicable terms.
  5. Where a decision is to Approve or Partially Approve a refund, the refund is processed automatically via Stripe. Where a decision is to Decline, you are informed of your escalation rights.
  6. A complete immutable audit record of the complaint lifecycle is created and retained for 7 years.

4.2 CEO oversight

The CEO of AI Lane Limited is notified of all complaint activity and retains the authority to override any ADRA decision at any stage. This oversight is not optional: it is a structural feature of the complaint architecture. If you are dissatisfied with a decision and wish to request CEO review, state this clearly in your follow-up communication.

4.3 Your right to human review

If you wish to request that a human reviews your complaint rather than, or in addition to, ADRA, you may state this in your complaint submission or follow-up. We will accommodate reasonable requests for human review, particularly for complex or sensitive complaints.

§7 Escalation

7.1 Internal escalation

If you are not satisfied with the initial ADRA response, you may escalate your complaint to senior human review by replying to your complaint response and stating that you wish to escalate. We will acknowledge your escalation request within 2 business days. The escalated review will be conducted by a human reviewer and a revised decision will be issued within 10 business days of the escalation request.

7.2 Where we cannot resolve your complaint

If, after exhausting our internal complaint process, you remain dissatisfied with the outcome, you may pursue resolution through:

  • Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) — we will indicate the relevant approved ADR scheme in our final response letter where applicable.
  • Legal proceedings in the courts of England and Wales.
  • For data protection matters — the Information Commissioner's Office (see §9).

7.3 Final response letter

Where we consider a complaint to be fully addressed and no further internal review is available, we will issue a final response letter confirming this and setting out your remaining options. Our internal complaint process is typically concluded within 8 weeks of the initial complaint submission.

§8 Data Protection Complaints

Complaints relating to how we handle your personal data are treated separately from general service complaints. If you raise a data protection concern, it will be:

  • Triaged immediately and escalated to the data controller within 24 hours.
  • Assessed against our obligations under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.
  • Responded to within the statutory one-calendar-month timescale required by UK GDPR.

For data protection complaints, please contact: [email protected]

§9 ICO Referral Right

If you have raised a data protection complaint with us and are not satisfied with our response, you have the right at any time to refer your complaint to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), the UK supervisory authority for data protection.

§10 Your Consumer Rights

Nothing in this Complaint Handling Policy affects or limits your statutory rights under:

  • The Consumer Rights Act 2015 — including rights in relation to digital content that is not of satisfactory quality, not fit for purpose, or not as described.
  • The Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013.
  • The UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.
  • Any other applicable consumer protection legislation.

Where any provision of this Complaint Handling Policy conflicts with your statutory rights, your statutory rights prevail.

§11 Professional Legal Advice

If your complaint concerns the content of an Assessment Output and its implications for an employment matter, we strongly recommend that you seek independent legal advice from a solicitor or barrister qualified to practise in England and Wales before making any employment-related decisions. Ailane Assessment Outputs do not constitute legal advice, and we are not able to advise you on the merits of any employment law matter, claim, or dispute.

If you are an employee or worker and believe you may have an employment law claim, you may wish to seek advice from:

  • A solicitor or barrister experienced in employment rights.
  • ACAS (Advisory, Conciliation and Arbitration Service) — acas.org.uk · 0300 123 1100.
  • Citizens Advice — citizensadvice.org.uk.
  • Your trade union, if you are a member.

§12 Complaint Records

We retain records of all complaints, including the content of your complaint, any evidence submitted, all communications, ADRA decisions, and the complete audit trail of the complaint lifecycle, for a period of seven years from the date of closure of the complaint. This is consistent with our obligations and the limitation periods applicable to potential legal claims. Further details of how we handle this data are set out in our Privacy Policy.
