Methodology

How these statistics are produced

This page sets out the sources, the aggregate-only discipline, the publication floor, and the update cadence behind the UK Employment Tribunal Statistics.

Sources

The statistics are computed from two categories of published material:

  • Published UK employment tribunal decisions released by HM Courts & Tribunals Service and the judiciary.
  • Public regulator enforcement registers maintained by UK regulators and enforcement bodies.

Only material already in the public domain is used. Figures are counts and ratios computed over that published corpus.

Aggregate-only discipline

Published figures are aggregate statistics: counts, cohort totals, and ratios. No client data is included, and no individual case is singled out for identification through these pages.

Aggregate only
Statistics describe groups of decisions and employers in the aggregate. They are information about the published record, not advice about any particular organisation or matter.

Publication floor (PF-BM-1)

A cohort is published only when it clears the estate publication floor, PF-BM-1: at least 25 distinct employers and at least 10 decided cases. Cohorts below either threshold are held back from publication.

PF-BM-1 floor
Minimum 25 distinct employers and 10 decided cases per published cohort. Cohorts shown on the statistics page all meet this floor.

Update cadence

Statistics recompute from the live corpus at page load. Each figure reflects the corpus as it stood when the page was requested, and the computation date is shown alongside the headline figures.

Claim-category naming

Claim-category counts are labelled by ACEI category number. The display names for those categories are keyed to the ACEI Founding Constitution v1.0 taxonomy and are applied as a separate content step.
