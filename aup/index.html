AI Lane Limited, registered in England and Wales, Companies House 17035654 ("AI Lane"). Published at ailane.ai/aup/.

This Policy applies to business customers only. It forms part of the Master Services Agreement published at ailane.ai/msa/ (the "MSA") and binds the Customer and every Authorised User. Capitalised terms have the meanings given in the MSA.

1. Purpose and application

1.1 This Policy sets the rules for use of the Ailane platform, the Services, the Intelligence Products and the Outputs.

1.2 The Customer is responsible for the acts and omissions of its Authorised Users under this Policy.

1.3 This Policy is accepted by click-acceptance in the same way as the MSA, with each version identified by a version number and SHA-256 content hash.

1.4 Each Authorised User owes this Policy directly to AI Lane, as a contractual undertaking given by that user's click-acceptance at first login, and AI Lane may enforce it directly against that user (MSA clause 18.3). This is in addition to, and does not reduce, the Customer's responsibility under clause 1.2.

2. Permitted use and data classes

2.1 The Services may be used only: (a) for the Customer's lawful internal business purposes; (b) by Authorised Users, each through their own individual account; and (c) in accordance with the MSA, the DPA and this Policy.

2.2 Material obtained from the Services falls into three classes, with only the following express permissions (MSA clause 9):

  • (a) Knowledge Library Content — may be viewed, quoted and used for the Customer's internal research and compliance purposes, preserving attribution; systematic or bulk reproduction is prohibited (clause 5);
  • (b) Intelligence Data — internal business use only. It must not be transferred, disclosed, sold, licensed or otherwise provided to any third party in any form — including by screenshot, API relay or embedding in third-party systems — and must not be used to build or contribute to any competing dataset or service;
  • (c) Packs — may be shared with the Customer's Professional Advisers, under confidentiality, for advice on the Customer's own affairs. This is the only onward-sharing route; it covers the generated Pack documents themselves and never extends to underlying Intelligence Data in bulk (MSA clause 9.6).

2.3 As set out in MSA clause 9.7, the Customer may also: make material obtained from the Services available to its employees and contractors, under confidentiality, for its internal purposes; share it with an Affiliate only where the Affiliate is first bound in writing to the same restrictions; and disclose it where compelled by law or a regulator, with prompt notice to AI Lane where lawful. Any use, disclosure or transfer not expressly permitted is prohibited.

3. Unlawful and infringing use

3.1 The Services must not be used:

  • (a) for any unlawful purpose, or to store, submit or transmit unlawful, defamatory or deceptive content;
  • (b) to infringe the intellectual property, privacy or other rights of any person;
  • (c) to harass, threaten or harm any person;
  • (d) in breach of any export control, sanctions or anti-money-laundering law; or
  • (e) to submit special category or criminal offence personal data to the Services (MSA clause 8.1).

4. Security and access

4.1 Users must not:

  • (a) attempt unauthorised access to any account, system, network or data connected with the Services;
  • (b) probe, scan or test the security of the platform, or run penetration or vulnerability testing, without AI Lane's prior written consent;
  • (c) share, sell or transfer credentials, or allow anyone other than the named Authorised User to use an account;
  • (d) circumvent, disable or interfere with access controls, the organisation gate, acceptance mechanics or other security or integrity features; or
  • (e) introduce malware or other harmful code.

4.2 Suspected account compromise must be reported to AI Lane without delay (clause 11).

4.3 Multi-factor authentication is required for owner and administrator accounts; the Customer decides whether to require it for its other Authorised Users (MSA clause 8.2). A user who loses access to an enrolled factor may recover access through AI Lane's support route, subject to identity verification.

5. Protection of the platform and Intelligence Products

5.1 Users must not:

  • (a) bulk-scrape, harvest, or systematically extract or reproduce the Intelligence Products, the underlying intelligence corpus, or any substantial part of them, whether manually or by automated means;
  • (b) use robots, crawlers or comparable tools on the platform without AI Lane's prior written consent;
  • (c) reverse engineer, decompile or disassemble any part of the platform or Intelligence Products, except to the extent of rights that cannot lawfully be excluded (clause 5.3);
  • (d) resell, sublicense, republish or otherwise make the Services, Outputs, Knowledge Library Content or Intelligence Data available to third parties, or use them to provide a competing or derivative service, except as expressly permitted by MSA clause 9 (including sharing Packs with the Customer's Professional Advisers, under confidentiality, for advice on the Customer's own affairs); or
  • (e) remove or obscure proprietary notices or attribution.

5.2 The Intelligence Products are protected by intellectual property rights, including copyright and database rights.

5.3 Nothing in this clause 5 excludes or restricts rights that cannot lawfully be excluded, including: the acts permitted by sections 50A to 50D of the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988; a lawful user's rights under regulation 19(2) of the Copyright and Rights in Databases Regulations 1997 (SI 1997/3032); and the making of copies permitted by section 29A of the 1988 Act, any contract term purporting to restrict which is unenforceable (section 29A(5)). Nothing in this Policy restricts any person's independent use of public tribunal decisions obtained from official sources, including under the Open Government Licence.

6. No legal advice; no misrepresentation

6.1 Outputs are business information and intelligence. They are not legal advice, and no solicitor–client or other adviser–client relationship arises from their use (MSA clause 3).

6.2 Users must not present, market or otherwise misrepresent Outputs — internally or to any third party — as legal advice, or as advice given by AI Lane.

6.3 The Customer should take independent professional advice where appropriate before acting on Outputs (MSA clause 3.3).

7. Automated decisions, human review and individuals

7.1 Users must not use Outputs to take a decision based entirely on automated processing that produces a legal effect concerning an individual, or has a similarly significant effect for an individual, except in accordance with Articles 22A to 22D UK GDPR (as inserted by the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025).

7.2 Before any decision materially affecting an individual is taken in reliance on Outputs, a person with authority and competence to depart from the Output must review it and exercise their own judgement. This Policy is deliberately stricter than the minimum the law requires.

7.3 A Customer that takes significant decisions based entirely on automated processing must itself operate the safeguards required by Article 22C UK GDPR, including providing information about such decisions and enabling the individual to make representations, obtain human intervention and contest the decision.

7.4 Outputs and Intelligence Data must not be used to identify, profile or make any decision about an individual tribunal claimant, witness or representative.

7.5 The Services must not be used to vet job applicants or workers by reference to prior tribunal claims or other protected acts, or otherwise in any way that would victimise a person (Equality Act 2010, sections 27 and 111–112), subject a worker to detriment for protected disclosures (Employment Rights Act 1996, sections 47B and 104), or discriminate against any person. Breach of this clause 7 is a material breach for the purposes of MSA clause 14.1(c).

8. AI feature integrity

8.1 In relation to AI-assisted features, users must not:

  • (a) attempt prompt-injection attacks or otherwise manipulate, bypass or subvert the features' instructions, safety controls or intended operation;
  • (b) attempt to extract system prompts, configurations, model weights or the underlying models; or
  • (c) use the features to generate content that would breach clause 3.

9. Fair usage

9.1 Usage that is excessive or abusive relative to the subscribed Tier — including usage that imposes a disproportionate load on the platform, distorts analytics, or circumvents Tier boundaries — is not permitted. AI Lane may publish rate or usage limits for each Tier on ailane.ai; any published limits form part of this Policy.

9.2 Where practicable, AI Lane will contact the Customer about unusual but apparently good-faith usage before treating it as a breach of this Policy.

10. Breach and enforcement

10.1 AI Lane applies a graduated response to breach of this Policy, acting proportionately to the seriousness and persistence of the breach:

  • (a) Warning — notice of the breach and the action required;
  • (b) Suspension — suspension of the affected accounts or of the organisation's access; suspension operates fail-closed, mirroring the platform: access remains locked until the breach is resolved and any required re-acceptance is completed (MSA clause 14, including data-export access and continuing DPA assistance under MSA clause 14.4); then
  • (c) Termination — for serious or repeated breach where suspension has not resolved the matter (MSA clause 15).

10.2 AI Lane may suspend immediately, without a prior warning, where reasonably necessary to address a serious security, legal or integrity risk.

10.3 AI Lane may preserve and use records of the breach (including platform logs and Acceptance Records) as evidence.

11. Reporting and complaints

11.1 Suspected breaches of this Policy, account compromise or security vulnerabilities should be reported to AI Lane at the abuse and security reporting mailbox published at ailane.ai.

11.2 Reports should include enough detail to investigate. Reporting a vulnerability does not authorise security testing (clause 4.1(b)).

11.3 Complaints about AI Lane's processing of personal data follow AI Lane's published complaints process at ailane.ai/complaints/ (the route provided for by section 103 of the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025). That route is separate from the abuse-report mailbox in clause 11.1.

12. Changes to this Policy

12.1 Changes to this Policy follow the change mechanism in MSA clause 5: new versions are published with a version number and content hash; material changes require re-acceptance; re-locking is confined to changes required by law, and MSA clauses 5.4 and 14.4 govern declined changes and any re-lock period.

13. Interpretation

13.1 The examples in this Policy are illustrative, not exhaustive. Whether use breaches this Policy is assessed reasonably by AI Lane in light of its purpose: keeping the Services lawful, secure and reliable for all customers.

13.2 This Policy is part of the MSA and is governed by the law of England and Wales, with the courts of England and Wales having exclusive jurisdiction.
