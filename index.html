Statute Overview

The foundational statute of UK employment law. Consolidates individual employment rights covering written particulars, protection from dismissal, redundancy, and key worker protections.

  • Written statement of employment particulars — s.1 day-one obligation
  • Unfair dismissal rights — qualifying period, fair reasons, fair procedure
  • Redundancy payment entitlement — statutory calculation and qualifying periods
  • Protection from detriment — whistleblowing, health & safety, jury service
  • Flexible working rights — s.80F statutory right to request from day one (Apr 2024)
  • Sunday trading and shop worker protections

Key Sections — Compliance Obligations
ERA 1996 s.1
Written Statement of Particulars
Must be provided on or before the first day of employment. Covers: name, start date, pay, hours, holiday, notice, job title, place of work, pension, sick pay, and collective agreements. Failure creates RRI Contractual Conformity evidence gap.
ERA 1996 s.94–s.134
Unfair Dismissal Framework
Requires 2 years' continuous service (with exceptions). Dismissal must satisfy a statutory fair reason and a fair procedure. Automatic unfair dismissal applies regardless of service for protected activities. Core ACEI Category 1 driver.
ERA 1996 s.139–s.181
Redundancy Payments
Statutory redundancy pay based on age, service, and weekly pay (capped annually). Selection must be fair and non-discriminatory. ACEI Category 6 core driver.
ERA 1996 s.43A–s.43L
Protected Disclosures (Whistleblowing)
Workers making qualifying disclosures in the public interest are protected from detriment and dismissal. Compensation is uncapped. Policy must be documented and communicated. ACEI Category 4.
ERA 1996 s.80F
Flexible Working Requests
Right to request from day one of employment (amended April 2024). Employer must genuinely consider and respond within 2 months. Must be referenced in employee handbooks and RRI Policy Alignment evidence.
Full clause-level analysis and remediation guidance available at Operational tier and above. Governance tier includes cross-reference to all 47 instruments and RRI pillar mapping.

6 CCI Conduct Components
25%
Tribunal Claim Frequency (TCF)
Claims per 100 employees, recency-weighted (Y1: 45%, Y2: 35%, Y3: 20%). Sector-calibrated bands. Bayesian credibility weighting applied where claim volume is low (N < 50).
20%
Adverse Outcome Ratio (AOR)
Adverse outcomes (judgments, settlements, defaults) as a proportion of total claims, recency-weighted. AOR 0% = 100; AOR 100% = 0. Weight redistributed if zero claims.
20%
Payout Severity Index (PSI)
Total payouts per 100 employees, log-scaled to prevent outlier distortion from a single catastrophic award. Sector-calibrated. Credibility-weighted for low-claim enterprises.
15%
Regulatory Enforcement Events (REE)
Formal enforcement actions by qualifying authorities (HSE, ICO, EHRC, HMRC). Improvement notices, prohibition notices, penalty notices, published outcomes. Each event is individually material — no credibility adjustment applied.
10%
Governance Responsiveness (GR)
Timeliness and completeness of responses to formal complaints, grievances, and regulatory inquiries. Simplified methodology for enterprises under 100 employees. Never redistributed; non-reporting receives GR = 0.
10%
Repeat Allegation Pattern (RAP)
Whether allegation types recur across ACEI categories, indicating structural governance weakness. Severity-weighted: Critical categories (Cat 2, 4, 10, 11) weight 2.0×; Standard categories 1.0×.
