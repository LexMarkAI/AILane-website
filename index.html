Engine architecture

The check engine runs a multi-pass analysis. Structural extraction identifies clause boundaries. Semantic matching aligns each clause to the relevant statutory provisions using legal-domain embeddings. A severity classifier grades each finding against a five-tier taxonomy — from Critical (day-one statutory breach) through to Advisory (best-practice divergence). Every finding is traced back to the source statute and, where available, the leading appellate authority.

Severity taxonomy

  • Critical — Day-one statutory breach. Immediate tribunal exposure.
  • Severe — Conditional breach (e.g. triggers on length-of-service milestone).
  • Material — Likely non-compliance under prevailing case law; defence possible but weak.
  • Forward — Compliant today, non-compliant once upcoming provision commences.
  • Advisory — Good-practice divergence; no statutory breach but recommended remediation.

PDF report structure

Executive summary. Finding-by-finding detail with clause quotation, statutory reference, case authority, severity, remediation recommendation. Forward exposure appendix. Exposure Score (for subscribers). Signed, dated, and timestamped for your records.

The constitutional boundary

Ailane assembles requirements. Your solicitor drafts the clauses. That line is deliberate. Drafting is the regulated legal service — it carries professional liability, it requires SRA authorisation, and it is properly performed by a qualified solicitor familiar with your organisation. What Ailane does is the research layer that sits underneath: the 401 provisions that apply, the 240 tribunal decisions that interpret them, the ACAS guidance and HSE requirements that complete the picture. We do the intelligence work. Your solicitor does the drafting work. The division is constitutional — not commercial.
