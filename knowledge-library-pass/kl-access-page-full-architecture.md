**Purpose:** Answer "what is this?" in 30 seconds of reading.

**Copy direction:**

The Ailane Knowledge Library is a UK employment law research platform. It contains every major piece of employment legislation — mapped provision by provision — alongside leading case law, authoritative guidance from ACAS, HSE, ICO, EHRC, and HMRC, and real-time legislative change tracking.

At its centre is Eileen, your AI employment law guide. She doesn't just search the library — she understands it. **What you can do:**
- Ask Eileen about any UK employment law obligation — SSP, unfair dismissal, discrimination, holiday pay, TUPE, redundancy, flexible working, whistleblowing, and more
- Research specific legislative instruments — read provisions, understand amendments, track commencement dates

- Track legislative changes — the Employment Rights Act 2025 provisions are tracked through commencement with dates and impact analysis
- Understand your sector's exposure — Eileen can explain which obligations apply to your industry and workforce structure

### SECTION 3: HOW EILEEN WORKS — Building Confidence

**Purpose:** Help the visitor understand who Eileen is, how she thinks, and why they can trust her. This is the "get to know the salesperson" section.

**Copy direction:**

**Eileen follows a five-step approach to every question:**

1. **Acknowledge** — she confirms she understands what you're asking. "Let's look at your SSP obligations under the new rules."

2. **Contextualise** — she places your question in its legal framework. "Under the Employment Rights Act 2025, SSP was reformed effective 6 April 2026."

3. **Educate** — she explains what the law requires. "The three-day waiting period has been abolished. SSP is now payable from the first qualifying day of incapacity."

4. **Connect** — she explains what this means for you. "For employers in facilities management with high sickness absence rates, this change will increase SSP costs from day one of every absence."

5. **Invite** — she offers to go deeper. "I can show you the full provision in the Knowledge Library, or we can look at how this affects your specific contracts."

**What Eileen will always do:**
- Trace every answer to its statutory source (legislation.gov.uk reference)
- Identify the specific provision that applies (e.g., ERA 2025 s.21)
- Explain in plain English what the obligation means for employers
- Reference relevant case law where it exists
- Signpost professional legal advice — "For advice specific to your situation, an employment solicitor would be best placed"

**What Eileen will never do:**
- Give legal advice — she provides regulatory intelligence to inform your decisions
- Tell you "you are compliant" or "you are not compliant" — she identifies where your position aligns with or diverges from statutory requirements
- Promise outcomes — she provides intelligence, not guarantees
- Make up answers — if something isn't in her library, she'll say so honestly

**Constitutional governance:** Every Eileen response is governed by three founding constitutions (ACEI v1.0, RRI v1.0, CCI v1.0) and the intelligence/advice boundary defined in PLUGIN-001 Art. XIV. This is not a marketing claim — the constitutional framework is ratified, version-controlled, and auditable.

### SECTION 8: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**"Is this legal advice?"**
No. Ailane provides regulatory intelligence — information about what the law requires, traced to its statutory source. It does not advise you on what to do about it. For legal advice specific to your situation, consult a qualified employment solicitor. Eileen will always signpost this.

**"How is this different from just reading legislation.gov.uk?"**
legislation.gov.uk gives you the raw text of the law. The Ailane Knowledge Library gives you Eileen — who reads the raw text, understands how provisions interact, maps case law to statutory requirements, and explains what it all means for employers in plain English. She's the difference between having a law library and having a guide who knows every book in it.

**"What if Eileen doesn't know the answer?"**
She'll tell you. Eileen is honest about the boundaries of her knowledge. If something isn't in the library, she'll say "That's not in the Ailane Knowledge Library" and point you to where you might find it. She never makes up answers.

**"Can I use this for a tribunal case?"**
Eileen provides regulatory intelligence that can inform your understanding of the legal position. She does not provide evidence, expert opinions, or legal arguments for tribunal proceedings. For tribunal representation, you need a qualified employment solicitor or representative.

**"Is my data safe?"**
Ailane is registered with the ICO (Reg. No. 00013389720) and operates under UK GDPR. Your session data is processed for the duration of your access window and handled in accordance with our privacy policy. Per-session users do not need to create an account.

**"What happens when my access window expires?"**
Your session ends. If you purchased add-on reports (transcript, advisory, project summary), those are delivered to your email and available for 90 days. Your conversation history is not retained after the session unless you purchase a transcript.
