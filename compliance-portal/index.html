⚠ CRITICAL
Clause 4 · Dismissal Provisions
Day-one unfair dismissal rights not reflected
ERA 2025 s.22 removes the two-year qualifying period for unfair dismissal claims with effect from commencement. This contract still references the qualifying period and is non-compliant from the commencement date.
Remediation: Remove all references to qualifying periods for unfair dismissal. Replace with language confirming the right applies from day one of employment. Update your contract template before the ERA 2025 commencement date for this provision.
→ ERA 2025 s.22 · Employment Rights Act 1996 s.94 (as amended) · ACEI CAT 01
⚠ CRITICAL
Clause 9 · Flexible Working
Flexible working default right absent
ERA 2025 s.118 establishes flexible working as a default right from day one of employment. The contract makes no provision for this. Refusal must be based on one of the eight statutory grounds and must be documented.
Remediation: Add a clause acknowledging the employee's right to request flexible working from day one. Include a reference to the employer's flexible working policy and the obligation to respond within the statutory timeframe (two months). Document all refusal grounds.
→ ERA 2025 s.118 · Employment Rights Act 1996 s.80F (as amended) · ACEI CAT 07
⚠ CRITICAL
Clause 14 · Hours and Zero Hours
Guaranteed hours obligation not addressed
ERA 2025 s.1 introduces a right to guaranteed hours for workers whose hours exceed a reference period average. If this contract applies to variable-hours staff, the obligation to offer guaranteed hours must be addressed.
Remediation: If applicable to zero or variable-hours workers, add provisions addressing the guaranteed hours framework. If this contract applies to guaranteed-hours employees only, add a clause confirming this explicitly to remove ambiguity.
→ ERA 2025 s.1 · ACEI CAT 09
ⓘ WARNING
Clause 6 · Leave Entitlements
Bereavement leave entitlement not reflected
ERA 2025 s.73A introduces a statutory entitlement to bereavement leave. The contract's leave provisions do not reference this entitlement, creating a potential gap and a risk of grievance if employees are unaware of the right.
Remediation: Add a bereavement leave clause referencing the statutory entitlement and any enhanced company provision. Cross-reference your staff handbook if the detail is held there.
→ ERA 2025 s.73A · ACEI CAT 07
ⓘ WARNING
Clause 17 · Trade Union Rights
Updated trade union access rights not acknowledged
ERA 2025 strengthens trade union access rights and introduces new protections against detriment for union activities. The contract's existing clause does not reflect the updated statutory position.
Remediation: Update the trade union clause to reflect current statutory rights under ERA 2025. Confirm no provisions in the contract could be construed as discouraging union membership or activities.
