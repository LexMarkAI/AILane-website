AI Lane Limited, registered in England and Wales, Companies House 17035654 ("AI Lane"). Published at ailane.ai/dpa/.

This DPA applies to business customers only. It forms part of the Master Services Agreement published at ailane.ai/msa/ (the "MSA") and is incorporated into it by reference.

1. Background and scope

1.1 This DPA governs AI Lane's processing of Customer Personal Data in providing the Services. It is intended to meet the requirements of Article 28(3) of the UK GDPR.

1.2 Capitalised terms not defined here have the meanings given in the MSA.

2. Definitions

2.1 "UK GDPR", "controller", "processor", "data subject", "personal data", "personal data breach" and "processing" have the meanings given in UK data protection law (the UK GDPR as defined in section 3(10) of the Data Protection Act 2018, read with section 205(4)).

2.2 "Customer Personal Data" means personal data submitted to the Services by or on behalf of the Customer and processed by AI Lane on the Customer's behalf, as described in Annex 1.

2.3 "Data Protection Laws" means the UK GDPR, the Data Protection Act 2018 (including amendments made by the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025) and, where applicable, the Privacy and Electronic Communications (EC Directive) Regulations 2003.

3. Roles of the parties

3.1 Customer Personal Data — Customer is controller; AI Lane is processor. For organisation and user data submitted through onboarding and use of the Services, the Customer is the controller and AI Lane is the processor, processing only as described in this DPA.

3.2 Tribunal-derived intelligence corpus — AI Lane is an independent controller. For its compliance-intelligence corpus derived from public tribunal decisions and related public sources, and the analytical products built on that corpus, AI Lane determines its own purposes and means. That processing is not carried out on the Customer's behalf and sits outside the processor scope of this DPA.

3.3 Optional profile fields. Where the Customer chooses to provide optional profile fields (for example SIC code, headcount or jurisdiction), AI Lane processes them: (a) as processor, to operate the Customer's account and Tier features; and (b) as an independent controller, where those fields feed AI Lane's intelligence analytics, as described in the Privacy Notice published on ailane.ai.

3.4 AI Lane's own records. AI Lane is an independent controller of its account administration, billing and agreement-acceptance records (including Acceptance Records kept for legal and audit purposes), and of its own site analytics on ailane.ai, as described in its Privacy Notice.

3.5 Each party will comply with Data Protection Laws in its respective role. The Customer is responsible for the lawfulness of the Customer Personal Data it submits and of the instructions it gives.

4. Processing on documented instructions — Art. 28(3)(a)

4.1 AI Lane will process Customer Personal Data only on the Customer's documented instructions, including as regards transfers to a third country, unless required to do otherwise by law to which AI Lane is subject; in that case AI Lane will inform the Customer of the legal requirement before processing, unless the law prohibits this on important grounds of public interest.

4.2 The Customer's documented instructions at acceptance are: the MSA, this DPA and the Customer's configuration and use of the platform. The Customer may give further documented instructions consistent with the Agreement, and AI Lane will comply with them; where an instruction requires material change to the Services, the parties will discuss in good faith any resulting adjustment to the Fees, and clause 4.3 applies if AI Lane considers the instruction unlawful.

4.3 AI Lane will inform the Customer immediately if, in its opinion, an instruction infringes Data Protection Laws.

5. Confidentiality of processing personnel — Art. 28(3)(b)

5.1 AI Lane will procure that every person it authorises to process Customer Personal Data is committed to an obligation of confidentiality or is under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality.

6. Security of processing — Art. 28(3)(c)

6.1 AI Lane will implement and maintain technical and organisational measures appropriate to the risk, as required by Article 32 UK GDPR. The current measures are summarised in Annex 2.

6.2 Multi-factor authentication is required for owner and administrator accounts. The Customer decides whether to require multi-factor authentication for its other Authorised Users and should reflect that choice in its own risk assessment. A user who loses access to an enrolled factor may recover access through AI Lane's support route, subject to identity verification.

6.3 AI Lane will take steps to see that any person acting under its authority who has access to Customer Personal Data processes it only on the Customer's instructions, unless required by law.

7. Sub-processors — Art. 28(3)(d)

7.1 The Customer gives general written authorisation for the sub-processors listed in Annex 3.

7.2 AI Lane will give the Customer at least 30 days' advance written notice (by email or platform notice) of any intended addition or replacement of a sub-processor, giving the Customer the opportunity to object.

7.3 If the Customer objects on reasonable data-protection grounds within the notice period, the parties will discuss the concern in good faith. If the concern is not resolved, the Customer may terminate the affected Services by written notice and will receive a pro-rata refund of prepaid Fees for the unexpired remainder of the term (MSA clause 15.3).

7.4 AI Lane will impose on each sub-processor, by written contract, the same data protection obligations as are set out in this DPA, and remains liable to the Customer for the performance of each sub-processor's data protection obligations.

8. Data subject rights — Art. 28(3)(e)

8.1 Taking into account the nature of the processing, AI Lane will assist the Customer by appropriate technical and organisational measures, insofar as this is possible, in fulfilling the Customer's obligation to respond to requests to exercise data subject rights under UK GDPR Chapter III (including access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection and rights relating to automated decision-making under Articles 22A to 22D). This assistance continues during any suspension or re-lock (MSA clause 14.4).

8.2 If AI Lane receives such a request directly, it will pass it to the Customer without undue delay and will not respond substantively except on the Customer's documented instruction or where legally required.

9. Personal data breach — Art. 28(3)(f)

9.1 AI Lane will notify the Customer without undue delay after becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting Customer Personal Data.

9.2 The notification will include, so far as the information is available to AI Lane (and may be provided in phases), the information the Customer reasonably requires to meet its obligations under Articles 33 and 34 UK GDPR.

9.3 AI Lane will take reasonable steps to contain and remediate the breach. Notification is not an admission of fault by either party.

10. Assistance with Articles 32 to 36 — Art. 28(3)(f)

10.1 Taking into account the nature of the processing and the information available to it, AI Lane will assist the Customer with the Customer's obligations under Articles 32 to 36 UK GDPR (security of processing, breach notification to the Information Commissioner, communication to data subjects, data protection impact assessments and prior consultation).

11. Deletion or return — Art. 28(3)(g)

11.1 At the end of the provision of the Services, AI Lane will, at the Customer's choice, delete or return all Customer Personal Data, following the data export window in MSA clause 16.2, and will delete existing copies unless the law of England and Wales (or other applicable UK law) requires storage.

11.2 AI Lane may retain the records it holds as an independent controller under clause 3.4 (including Acceptance Records) for legal and audit purposes, as described in its Privacy Notice. Agreement-acceptance evidence is retained for six years after termination of the Agreement, reflecting the limitation period for contract claims (Limitation Act 1980, section 5).

11.3 The acceptance ledger is append-only. Erasure requests touching ledger entries are assessed under Article 17(3)(e) UK GDPR (retention necessary for the establishment, exercise or defence of legal claims); where that ground applies, the entry is retained for the period in clause 11.2.

12. Information and audit — Art. 28(3)(h)

12.1 AI Lane will make available to the Customer the information necessary to demonstrate compliance with the obligations in Article 28 UK GDPR and this DPA.

12.2 In the first instance AI Lane will provide written information and, as and when attained, summaries or copies of third-party certifications and audit reports (for example UK Cyber Essentials, SOC 2, ISO/IEC 27001, ISO/IEC 42001). AI Lane is on a certification route and does not currently claim to hold any of these certifications; reports will be shared if and when a certification is attained.

12.3 Where the materials in clause 12.2 are not sufficient to demonstrate compliance, AI Lane will allow for and contribute to an audit or inspection by the Customer (or an independent auditor mandated by the Customer that is not a competitor of AI Lane), conducted: on reasonable prior written notice; no more than once in any 12-month period, unless required by the Information Commissioner or following a personal data breach; during business hours; without unreasonable disruption to AI Lane's operations; subject to confidentiality; and at the Customer's cost.

13. International transfers

13.1 Primary hosting of Customer Personal Data (database, authentication and functions) is in the EU (Frankfurt, eu-central-1). Transfers from the UK to the EEA rely on the UK's adequacy regulations for the EEA.

13.2 AI Lane will not transfer Customer Personal Data outside the UK except in accordance with Chapter V of the UK GDPR and the Customer's documented instructions (clause 4).

13.3 Some sub-processors process personal data in, or accessible from, the United States. The transfer mechanism for each sub-processor is stated in its Annex 3 row: (a) Anthropic — the EU Standard Contractual Clauses with the UK International Data Transfer Addendum, as incorporated in Anthropic's data processing terms; and (b) Stripe — the UK Extension to the EU–US Data Privacy Framework while Stripe holds a live certification covering the relevant services, falling back to the UK Addendum to the EU Standard Contractual Clauses as incorporated in Stripe's data processing terms. Each restricted transfer is supported by a transfer risk assessment.

14. Liability

14.1 The exclusions and limitations of liability in MSA clause 13 apply to this DPA, and liability arising under or in connection with this DPA counts towards the cap in MSA clause 13.3.

14.2 Nothing in this DPA excludes or limits any liability that cannot lawfully be excluded or limited, and nothing affects a data subject's statutory rights.

15. General

15.1 If there is a conflict between this DPA and the MSA in relation to the processing of personal data, this DPA prevails (MSA clause 18.2).

15.2 Changes to this DPA follow the change mechanism in MSA clause 5 (versioned and content-hashed publication; re-acceptance for material changes; re-locking confined to changes required by law; MSA clauses 5.4 and 14.4 govern declined changes and processing during any re-lock).

15.3 This DPA and any dispute or claim arising out of or in connection with it are governed by the law of England and Wales, and the courts of England and Wales have exclusive jurisdiction.

Annex 1 — Processing details (Art. 28(3) particulars)

  • Subject matter: provision of the Ailane compliance-intelligence platform (the Services) to the Customer.
  • Duration: the Subscription Term, plus the data export window in MSA clause 16.2, followed by deletion or return under clause 11.
  • Nature and purpose: hosting and storage; identity and access management; onboarding and organisation-gate administration; recording and evidencing agreement acceptances; operating AI-assisted analysis features — prompts submitted to, and outputs returned from, those features are logged for service operation, security and abuse prevention; billing and payment administration; customer support and service communications.
  • Categories of data subjects: the Customer's directors, employees, contractors and other Authorised Users; Customer representatives named in organisation records; billing contacts; individuals (including the Customer's workers) referred to in unstructured text submitted to AI-assisted features.
  • Categories of personal data: names and business contact details; account identifiers and credentials; organisation profile data (including Companies House number, ALIN assignment and, where volunteered, optional profile fields such as SIC code, headcount and jurisdiction); agreement-acceptance evidence (version, SHA-256 content hash, server-side timestamp, IP address, user agent, accepting user's role); onboarding state; usage and device data; prompt and output logs from AI-assisted features, which may include personal data of the Customer's workers contained in submitted unstructured text; billing contact data. Payment card data is handled by Stripe as payment processor.
  • Special categories: none intended. The Services are not designed for special category or criminal offence data, and the Customer must not submit it (MSA clause 8.1).
  • Obligations and rights of the controller: as set out in the MSA and this DPA; the Customer instructs under clause 4 and exercises its audit and information rights under clause 12 (Article 28(3) UK GDPR).

Annex 2 — Technical and organisational measures (summary)

  1. Encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS) for platform traffic.
  2. Primary database, authentication and functions hosted with Supabase in the EU (Frankfurt, eu-central-1).
  3. Row-level security (RLS) access controls in the database.
  4. Append-only agreement-acceptance ledger; acceptance evidence stamped with version, SHA-256 content hash, server-side timestamp, IP address, user agent and role.
  5. Fail-closed onboarding and access gates: access remains locked until required acceptances are complete.
  6. Access controls for Customer users; multi-factor authentication is required for owner and administrator accounts, with factor recovery through AI Lane's support route (clause 6.2).
  7. AI sub-processors are contractually restricted from using Customer Personal Data to train foundation models.
  8. Certification route: UK Cyber Essentials, SOC 2 Type II, ISO/IEC 27001 and ISO/IEC 42001 are objectives; none is currently held or claimed (clause 12.2).
