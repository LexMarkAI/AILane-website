§2 About the Service

Ailane is an AI-assisted employment contract compliance analysis service. The Service analyses employment contract documents submitted by users against current UK statutory requirements, including but not limited to those arising under the Employment Rights Act 1996, the National Minimum Wage Act 1998, the Working Time Regulations 1998, and the Equality Act 2010.

The Service produces an Assessment Output identifying potential areas of concern, statutory gaps, and compliance observations based on the document submitted and the AI assessment engine's analysis of those documents against applicable legal requirements. Assessment Outputs are produced automatically and are not reviewed by a human before delivery.

The Service is provided from the United Kingdom and is designed for use in relation to employment relationships governed by the law of England and Wales, Scotland, or Northern Ireland.

Early Access. The Service is currently in Early Access. This means the platform is fully operational and accepting paid transactions, but is in the early phase of its commercial deployment. Assessment outputs reflect the current state of the AI assessment engine and the underlying dataset of UK employment tribunal decisions. The engine and dataset are subject to ongoing development. Your statutory rights under the Consumer Rights Act 2015 are not affected.

§5 Employer and Worker Scans

The Service operates two distinct access points:

5.1 Employer-Facing Service (ailane.ai/contract-scan/)

The employer-facing page is designed for organisations and individuals acting in the capacity of an employer or HR professional. Assessments submitted via this page are classified with a scan_audience value of "employer" in your Scan Record. The assessment framing, findings presentation, and any referral routing associated with your scan reflect this classification.

5.2 Worker-Facing Service (ailane.ai/contract-check-worker/)

The worker-facing page is designed for individuals who wish to assess their own employment contract against applicable statutory requirements. Assessments submitted via this page are classified with a scan_audience value of "worker" in your Scan Record. The assessment framing and findings presentation are directed at the individual in an employment relationship.

5.3 Same Assessment Engine

Both access points use the same underlying AI assessment engine and produce Assessment Outputs against the same set of statutory compliance requirements. The classification applies to how findings are framed and presented, and to downstream referral routing where applicable, not to the substantive analysis performed.

5.4 Document Scope

The Single Contract Check (£19) covers a single employment contract document. You should submit the document most relevant to the compliance question you wish to address. The Assessment Output is specific to the document submitted.

§8 Nature of Assessment Outputs

8.1 Not Legal Advice

Assessment Outputs produced by the Service do not constitute legal advice, legal opinion, or a statement of the law. They are AI-assisted analytical outputs identifying areas of a document that may warrant attention in light of applicable statutory requirements. They should not be relied upon as a definitive statement of an employer's legal obligations or a worker's legal rights.

8.2 AI-Assisted Analysis

The Service uses artificial intelligence to analyse documents. AI systems can make errors, misinterpret documents, fail to identify relevant provisions, or produce outputs that are incomplete or inaccurate. The accuracy and completeness of an Assessment Output is dependent upon the quality and completeness of the document submitted, the capabilities of the AI assessment engine at the time of the assessment, and the scope of the statutory requirements checked.

8.3 Point-in-Time Analysis

Assessment Outputs reflect the state of applicable UK employment law and statutory requirements as understood by the assessment engine at the time of analysis. Employment law is subject to change through legislation, case law, and regulatory guidance. An Assessment Output does not account for changes in the law occurring after the assessment engine's last update.

8.4 No Warranty of Completeness

We do not warrant that an Assessment Output will identify every potential compliance concern, statutory gap, or legal risk present in a document. The absence of a finding in an Assessment Output does not constitute confirmation that a document is legally compliant or free of risk.
