Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement

Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement between AI LANE LIMITED (Company No. 17035654; ICO Reg. No. 00013389720) ("Ailane") and THE RUDOLPH WALKER FOUNDATION (Registered Charity No. 1157221) ("the Foundation"), for the evaluation and conduct of a founding charity partnership for the Ailane Charity Suite (the "Purpose").

1. Confidential Information means non-public information disclosed by either party in connection with the Purpose, in any form, including platform architecture, commercial terms, the Foundation's operational information, and the content of this onboarding room; it excludes information that is or becomes public other than by breach, was lawfully held before disclosure, is independently developed, or is lawfully received from a third party without restriction.

2. Each party will use the other's Confidential Information only for the Purpose; protect it with at least reasonable care; disclose it only to trustees, directors, employees, volunteers and professional advisers who need it and are bound by confidence; and notify the other promptly of any unauthorised use or disclosure. Personal data is handled in accordance with UK GDPR; this agreement is not a data processing agreement.

3. Disclosure required by law, a court or a regulator (including the Charity Commission) is permitted to the extent required, with prior notice where lawful.

4. Obligations run for two years from the date of last acceptance and survive the end of discussions. No licence or transfer of intellectual property is granted. Either party may request return or destruction of its Confidential Information.

5. No obligation to proceed with any transaction arises. This is the entire agreement on its subject matter, varied only in writing, governed by the law of England and Wales with exclusive jurisdiction of its courts.

6. Execution: each signatory, authenticated by their invited email, completes the acceptance step in this room; the acceptance record (signatory, timestamp, document code and version) is that party's signature, effective as a simple electronic signature under the law of England and Wales.
