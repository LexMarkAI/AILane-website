Martyn's Law — protecting people at your events

Why this matters to the Foundation

the Terrorism (Protection of Premises) Act 2025 — Martyn's Law — asks venues and events to be ready to protect the public. The Drama Awards at the Millfield Theatre, the workshops, the fundraisers: any event where 200 or more people may be present (staff included) falls in the standard tier once the Act commences.

The duties
  • assess each event against the 200-person threshold;
  • have procedures for an attack — evacuation, invacuation, lockdown, communication;
  • make sure the people running the event know those procedures;
  • larger events (800+) carry enhanced requirements.
What we watch

the Act and the Home Office statutory guidance (published 15 April 2026), so your per-event readiness checklist always reflects the current expectations.

Key date commencement expected from 2027 — the Security Industry Authority will regulate; preparing now is the point.
