AI Lane Limited, registered in England and Wales, Companies House 17035654 ("AI Lane"). Published at ailane.ai/msa/.

This Agreement applies to business customers only. The Services are supplied solely to organisations acting in the course of business. AI Lane does not offer the Services to consumers.

1. Definitions

1.1 In this Agreement:

  • "Acceptance Record" — the record created when a user completes click-acceptance of an agreement version: the version number, the SHA-256 content hash of the published text, a server-side timestamp, the accepting user's IP address and user agent, and the accepting user's role.
  • "Affiliate" — any entity that directly or indirectly controls, is controlled by or is under common control with a party, where "control" means the power to direct that entity's management or affairs, by ownership, contract or otherwise.
  • "Agreement" — this Master Services Agreement together with the DPA, the AUP and the Order.
  • "AUP" — the Acceptable Use Policy published at ailane.ai/aup/.
  • "Authorised User" — an individual the Customer authorises to use the Services under its subscription.
  • "Business Day" — a day other than a Saturday, Sunday or public holiday in England.
  • "Customer" — the business organisation that accepts this Agreement.
  • "Customer Data" — data submitted to the Services by or on behalf of the Customer, including organisation profile data and Authorised User data.
  • "DPA" — the Data Processing Agreement published at ailane.ai/dpa/.
  • "Fees" — the charges payable for the Services under the Order.
  • "Intelligence Data" — tribunal enrichment records, RRI, CCI and ACEI scores, analytics, bulk exports and all other data obtained from the Services, in any form, other than Knowledge Library Content as displayed and Packs.
  • "Intelligence Products" — AI Lane's platform, software, compliance-intelligence corpus, datasets, indices, scores, models and analytical products, including intelligence derived from public tribunal decisions.
  • "Knowledge Library Content" — published case materials displayed through the Services for research, with attribution.
  • "Order" — the subscription details recorded through the platform's onboarding and payment process (Tier, Fees, billing frequency and term).
  • "Outputs" — the reports, analyses, scores, alerts and other content made available to the Customer through the Services, including Packs.
  • "Packs" — the compliance packs and solicitor packs generated by the Services for the Customer.
  • "Professional Advisers" — the Customer's solicitors, accountants, auditors and insurers, and its regulators where disclosure is required by law, in each case receiving information under confidentiality, for advice on the Customer's own affairs.
  • "Services" — the Ailane software-as-a-service platform and the compliance-intelligence rooms included in the Customer's Tier.
  • "Tier" — a subscription tier: Operational Readiness, Governance or Institutional.

2. The Services

2.1 AI Lane provides the Services on a subscription basis. Each Tier gives access to defined compliance-intelligence rooms as described on ailane.ai at the date of the Order.

2.2 AI Lane may develop and modify features of the Services, provided the Services remain materially as described for the subscribed Tier during the Subscription Term.

2.3 By accepting this Agreement the Customer confirms that it enters into it in the course of business and not as a consumer.

3. Information, not legal advice

3.1 The Services and the Outputs are business information and intelligence. They are not legal advice and are not a substitute for advice from a qualified professional adviser.

3.2 AI Lane is not a law firm, is not regulated as a legal services provider and does not carry on reserved legal activities. No solicitor–client relationship, and no other adviser–client relationship, arises under this Agreement or through use of the Services.

3.3 Outputs are generated from data and analytical models (including AI systems), may be incomplete or contain errors, and must be reviewed and assessed by the Customer before being acted on. The Customer remains solely responsible for its own decisions, obligations and compliance position, and should take independent professional advice where appropriate.

3.4 Nothing in the Services or the Outputs is a promise or prediction of any legal, regulatory or tribunal outcome.

4. Account, acceptance and authority

4.1 This Agreement is entered into by click-acceptance through the platform. Acceptance of this Agreement and the DPA, and any re-acceptance after a material change, may be completed only by the Customer's owner or administrator accounts. The accepting user warrants that they have authority to bind the Customer, and that user's role is recorded in the Acceptance Record. Acceptance by the Customer's owner account binds the Customer, and the platform's organisation gate opens only when the owner account has no outstanding required acceptances.

4.2 Each Authorised User must complete click-acceptance of the required agreements before that user's access is enabled, including the AUP, which each Authorised User undertakes directly to AI Lane from first login (AUP clause 1). Each acceptance creates an Acceptance Record held on an append-only ledger; a later acceptance of a newer version supersedes the earlier one. Acceptance Records are retained as evidence of agreement.

4.3 Each published version of this Agreement, the DPA and the AUP is identified by a version number and the SHA-256 content hash of its published text; the version displayed on the published page identifies the version in force.

4.4 The Customer is responsible for its Authorised Users' use of the Services, for keeping credentials confidential (see the AUP) and for the accuracy of its account information.

5. Changes to this Agreement

5.1 AI Lane may update this Agreement, the DPA and the AUP by publishing a new version (with a new version number and content hash) at the addresses stated above.

5.2 Changes that are not material take effect on publication, with notice given through the platform or by email.

5.3 Material changes require re-acceptance. Where a material change is required by law, publication may re-lock access for affected users until they accept the new version, and the organisation gate re-opens when the owner account has no outstanding acceptances. Re-locking is confined to changes required by law (clause 14.1(e)).

5.4 If the Customer declines a material change that is not required by law, the previously accepted version continues to govern until the end of the then-current Subscription Term period; alternatively, the Customer may terminate this Agreement by written notice and receive a pro-rata refund of prepaid Fees for the unexpired remainder of that period. Clause 14.4 applies during any re-lock.

6. Term and renewal

6.1 This Agreement starts when the Customer first accepts it, and the subscription runs for the initial term stated in the Order or, if none is stated, 12 months (the "Initial Term").

6.2 The subscription then renews for successive periods equal in length to the Initial Term (the Initial Term and all renewals together, the "Subscription Term"), unless either party gives written notice of non-renewal at least 30 days before the end of the then-current period.

7. Fees and payment

7.1 The Customer will pay the Fees stated in the Order, in advance at the billing frequency stated in the Order, collected by card through AI Lane's payment provider (Stripe) or as otherwise stated in the Order.

7.2 Invoices are payable within 30 days of the invoice date.

7.3 AI Lane may charge statutory interest and fixed compensation on overdue sums under the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998. Sums due under this clause sit outside the cap in clause 13.3.

7.4 Fees are exclusive of VAT, which the Customer will pay in addition where applicable, and, except where this Agreement or the law provides otherwise, are non-refundable.

8. Customer obligations

8.1 The Customer will: (a) use the Services only for lawful internal business purposes and in accordance with the AUP; (b) procure that each Authorised User complies with this Agreement; (c) provide accurate organisation information — including its Companies House number where requested — and keep it current; (d) apply appropriate human review before acting on Outputs, as required by clause 3 and the AUP; (e) not submit special category or criminal offence personal data to the Services; and (f) where it uses the Services or Outputs outside the United Kingdom, remain solely responsible for compliance with the laws of that jurisdiction governing that use.

8.2 The Customer is responsible for its own devices, connectivity and security practices. Multi-factor authentication is required for owner and administrator accounts; the Customer decides whether to require it for its other Authorised Users. A user who loses access to an enrolled factor may recover access through AI Lane's support route, subject to identity verification.

9. Intellectual property and data use

9.1 AI Lane and its licensors retain all intellectual property rights in the Services and the Intelligence Products. No rights are granted to the Customer except as expressly stated in this Agreement.

9.2 The Customer retains all rights in Customer Data and grants AI Lane a non-exclusive licence to host and process Customer Data to provide the Services and as set out in the DPA.

9.3 AI Lane grants the Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable licence, for the Subscription Term, to access the Services and to use Outputs, Knowledge Library Content and Intelligence Data for its internal business purposes, subject to clauses 9.4 to 9.8 and the AUP. Every permission in this clause 9 is express and exhaustive: any use, disclosure, transfer or onward provision of material obtained from the Services that is not expressly permitted by this Agreement is prohibited.

9.4 Knowledge Library Content. The Customer may view, quote and use Knowledge Library Content for its internal research and compliance purposes, preserving attribution. Systematic or bulk reproduction remains prohibited (AUP clause 5).

9.5 Intelligence Data. Intelligence Data may be used for the Customer's internal business purposes only. The Customer must not transfer, disclose, sell, license or otherwise provide Intelligence Data to any third party in any form — including by screenshot, API relay or embedding in third-party systems — and must not use it to build or contribute to any competing dataset or service.

9.6 Packs. The Customer may share Packs with its Professional Advisers, under confidentiality, for advice on the Customer's own affairs. This is the only onward-sharing route for material obtained from the Services; it covers the generated Pack documents themselves and never extends to underlying Intelligence Data in bulk.

9.7 Internal routes. The Customer may: (a) make material obtained from the Services available to its employees and contractors, under confidentiality, for the Customer's internal purposes; (b) share it with an Affiliate only where the Affiliate is first bound in writing to the same restrictions as apply to the Customer under this clause 9 and the AUP, the Customer remaining responsible for the Affiliate's compliance; and (c) disclose it where compelled by law or by a regulator, giving AI Lane prompt notice where lawful.

9.8 Outputs. As between the parties, AI Lane owns the Outputs. AI Lane grants the Customer a perpetual, non-exclusive, non-transferable licence to use the Outputs delivered to it during the Subscription Term for its internal business purposes. This licence survives termination and remains subject to clauses 9.3 to 9.7, clause 10 and the AUP, which continue to apply to delivered Outputs after termination.

9.9 Where the Customer chooses to provide optional profile fields (for example SIC code, headcount or jurisdiction), AI Lane may use them to operate and improve the Intelligence Products, as described in the DPA and the Privacy Notice published on ailane.ai.

10. Confidentiality

10.1 Each party will keep the other's Confidential Information confidential, use it only for the purposes of this Agreement, and disclose it only to personnel who need it and owe duties of confidence and, in the Customer's case, to its Professional Advisers, under confidentiality, for advice on the Customer's own affairs. Disclosure of material obtained from the Services is governed by clause 9, which prevails over this clause 10 for that material.

10.2 "Confidential Information" means non-public information disclosed under or in connection with this Agreement, including the Customer's business information and AI Lane's product, security and pricing information.

10.3 These duties do not apply to information that is or becomes public without breach, was lawfully held before disclosure, is independently developed, or must be disclosed by law or a regulator (with prompt notice to the other party where lawful). They survive termination.

11. Data protection

11.1 Each party will comply with applicable data protection law, including the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018. The DPA is incorporated into this Agreement by reference: it governs AI Lane's processing of Customer Personal Data as processor, records AI Lane's separate role as an independent controller of its tribunal-derived intelligence corpus, and contains the sub-processor register and international transfer terms.

12.1 Each party warrants that it has the authority to enter into this Agreement.

12.2 AI Lane warrants that it will supply the Services with reasonable skill and care and that the Services will operate materially as described for the subscribed Tier.

12.3 AI Lane does not warrant that the Services will be uninterrupted or without error, and no uptime or service-level commitment is given under this Agreement.

12.4 All other warranties, conditions and terms implied by statute or common law are excluded to the extent the law allows.

13. Liability and indemnities

13.1 Nothing in this Agreement excludes or limits liability for: (a) death or personal injury caused by negligence; (b) fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation; or (c) any other liability that cannot lawfully be excluded or limited.

13.2 Subject to clause 13.1, neither party is liable, however arising (contract, tort including negligence, breach of statutory duty or otherwise), for loss of profit or for any indirect or consequential loss.

13.3 Subject to clauses 13.1 and 13.2, each party's total aggregate liability arising under or in connection with this Agreement is limited to the greater of: (a) the total Fees paid or payable by the Customer to AI Lane in the 12 months immediately preceding the first event giving rise to the liability; and (b) £10,000. The cap applies to each party's aggregate liability and is applied before any set-off. Statutory interest and compensation under clause 7.3 sit outside, and do not erode, the cap. This cap does not limit the Customer's obligation to pay the Fees.

13.4 The parties, contracting as businesses, have considered this allocation of risk against the reasonableness requirement of the Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977, taking account of the nature and price of the Services, and consider it reasonable.

13.5 IP indemnity. AI Lane will indemnify the Customer against sums finally awarded, or agreed in settlement, on a third-party claim that the platform as provided by AI Lane, unmodified and used in accordance with this Agreement, infringes United Kingdom intellectual property rights. AI Lane may at its option modify or replace the affected element so that it is non-infringing or, failing that, terminate the affected Services and refund prepaid Fees for the unexpired remainder of the term. This indemnity does not apply to claims arising from combination with items not provided by AI Lane, from Customer Data or from use in breach of this Agreement; it is subject to the cap in clause 13.3; and it is conditional on prompt written notice, AI Lane having conduct of the defence and the Customer's reasonable cooperation.

13.6 Customer indemnity. The Customer will indemnify AI Lane against third-party claims — including claims brought by the Customer's own workers or Authorised Users — arising from the unlawfulness of Customer Data or from breach of the AUP by the Customer or an Authorised User, conditional on prompt written notice and the Customer being offered conduct of the defence.

14. Suspension

14.1 AI Lane may suspend access to all or part of the Services where: (a) the law requires it; (b) there is a serious security or integrity risk; (c) the Customer or an Authorised User is in material breach of the AUP; (d) undisputed Fees remain unpaid after written notice; or (e) a required re-acceptance of a material change required by law is outstanding (clauses 5.3 and 5.4).

14.2 Suspension for AUP breach operates fail-closed, mirroring the platform: access remains locked until the breach is resolved and any required re-acceptance is completed. AI Lane will act proportionately, suspend no more widely than reasonably necessary, and restore access promptly once the grounds for suspension end.

14.3 Suspension does not relieve the Customer of its payment obligations.

14.4 During any suspension or re-lock: the Customer retains access to Customer Data for export; AI Lane's assistance obligations under the DPA — including data subject rights assistance under Article 28(3)(e) UK GDPR — continue; and the last-accepted version of the DPA continues to govern processing.

15. Termination

15.1 Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately by written notice if the other: (a) commits a material breach and, where the breach is remediable, does not remedy it within 30 days of written notice; or (b) becomes insolvent or enters administration, liquidation, a company voluntary arrangement or any analogous step in any jurisdiction.

15.2 AI Lane may terminate immediately by written notice for serious or repeated breach of the AUP where suspension has not resolved the matter.

15.3 The Customer may terminate the affected Services following an unresolved sub-processor objection (DPA clause 7.3) and will receive a pro-rata refund of prepaid Fees for the unexpired remainder of the term. Non-renewal under clause 6.2 is not a breach of this Agreement.

16. Effect of termination

16.1 On expiry or termination, the Customer's access and subscription licences end (the delivered-Outputs licence in clause 9.8 survives) and each party's accrued rights and remedies are preserved.

16.2 For 30 days after expiry or termination, AI Lane will, on written request, make Customer Data available for export in a commonly used format.

16.3 After that window, AI Lane will delete or return Customer Personal Data in accordance with the DPA. AI Lane may retain Acceptance Records and billing and account records for legal and audit purposes, as independent controller, as described in the DPA.

16.4 Clauses that by their nature survive — including clauses 3, 9, 10, 11, 13 and 16 to 19 — continue after termination.

17. Notices

17.1 Notices must be in writing and in English, sent by email or given through the platform. Notices to AI Lane: the notices email address published at ailane.ai/msa/, or its registered office recorded at Companies House against number 17035654. Notices to the Customer: the owner account email held on the platform.

17.2 A notice sent by email or through the platform is deemed received on the next Business Day after sending, unless the sender receives evidence of non-delivery.

18. General

18.1 Entire agreement. This Agreement (including the DPA, the AUP and the Order) is the entire agreement between the parties for the Services and supersedes all prior discussions and arrangements on its subject matter. Each party confirms it has not relied on any statement not set out in this Agreement. Nothing in this clause excludes liability for fraudulent misrepresentation.

18.2 Precedence. If there is a conflict within the Agreement: the DPA prevails as to the processing of personal data; the Order prevails as to Tier, Fees, billing frequency and term; and otherwise this MSA prevails over the AUP and the Order.

18.3 Third-party rights. AI Lane may enforce the AUP directly against each Authorised User under that user's own undertaking to AI Lane (AUP clause 1). Subject to that, no person other than a party has any right under the Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999 to enforce any term of this Agreement, and the parties may vary or rescind this Agreement without the consent of any third party.

18.4 Assignment. The Customer may not assign or transfer this Agreement without AI Lane's prior written consent, not to be unreasonably withheld. AI Lane may assign this Agreement to a successor to its business on written notice.

18.5 Waiver and severance. A delay in enforcing a right is not a waiver of it. If any provision is found unenforceable, the remainder continues in effect.

18.6 Force majeure; relationship. Neither party is liable for failure or delay caused by events beyond its reasonable control; this does not excuse payment of sums properly due. The parties are independent contractors; this Agreement creates no partnership, joint venture or agency.
