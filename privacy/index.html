§5 Scan Record Data

5.1 What is a Scan Record

When you submit a document for assessment, we create a Scan Record. This record contains the document content, the Assessment Output, your payment reference, the scan_audience classification (employer or worker), the timestamp of submission, and technical metadata.

5.2 scan_audience

Every Scan Record includes a scan_audience field that records whether your scan was submitted via the employer-facing page (contract-scan/) or the worker-facing page (contract-check-worker/). This field is used for internal analytics, referral routing, and service improvement. It does not affect the substantive assessment performed.

5.3 Document Content

The document you submit may contain personal data about named individuals (for example, an employee named in a contract). We process this data solely to produce your Assessment Output. We do not use it for any other purpose and do not link it to any external dataset or profile.

5.4 Retention of Scan Records

Scan Records are retained for a period of 24 months from the date of the assessment. Following this period, Scan Records are deleted from our production systems. Anonymised and aggregated data derived from Scan Records (which does not identify individuals) may be retained indefinitely for platform improvement purposes.

§6 Worker-Specific Data Handling

Where you access the Service via the worker-facing page (contract-check-worker/), the following additional considerations apply:

6.1 Individual Rights Context

Documents submitted via the worker-facing page may be more likely to contain sensitive personal data relating to the submitting individual, such as details of their employment terms, pay, working hours, or contractual rights. We treat this data with the same care and security as all other Scan Record data. We do not share it with your employer or any third party without your express consent.

6.2 Referral Routing

Where the Service offers referral options to legal practitioners, worker-page scans are routed to claimant-side employment law practices. Any referral is voluntary and does not involve the disclosure of your Scan Record to a third party without your separate informed consent at the point of referral.

6.3 No Disclosure to Employers

We will not disclose the fact that you have used the worker-facing Service, or the content of your Scan Record, to your employer, former employer, or any person acting on behalf of your employer.

§7 Complaint and ADRA Data

7.1 What We Collect

When you raise a complaint, refund request, or dispute, we collect and process the following data: your contact details (if provided), the content of your complaint, evidence submitted in support of your complaint, communications between you and AI Lane Limited, ADRA decisions and reasoning, and a complete audit trail of the complaint lifecycle.

7.2 ADRA Processing Basis

Complaint data is processed by the ADRA on the basis of our legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f) UK GDPR) in managing complaints fairly, defending our legal position, maintaining accurate records, and improving the Service. Our legitimate interests are balanced against your interests and rights, and we consider this processing to be necessary and proportionate to those aims.

7.3 Retention of Complaint Data

Complaint records, including all associated communications, evidence, ADRA decisions, and audit trails, are retained for a period of seven years from the date of closure of the complaint. This retention period reflects potential limitation periods for legal claims and regulatory obligations. After seven years, complaint records are securely deleted.

7.4 Audit Trail Immutability

Audit trail records created in connection with complaint resolution are immutable: once created, they cannot be altered or deleted before the expiry of the retention period. This is necessary to maintain the integrity of the complaint record for legal and regulatory purposes.

§8 Analytics and Cookies

8.1 Google Analytics 4

We use Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to collect anonymised data about how visitors use our website, including pages visited, time on site, and general geographic location. GA4 data is used to improve the Service and understand user behaviour. GA4 uses cookies and similar technologies.

8.2 UTM Parameters

Where you arrive at the Service via a paid advertisement or tracked link, your UTM parameters (campaign source, medium, and audience classification) are recorded in sessionStorage and associated with your Scan Record for campaign performance measurement purposes. This data is anonymised for aggregate analytics reporting.

8.3 Essential Cookies

The Service uses essential session cookies necessary for the operation of the payment flow and scan delivery. These cookies do not track you across third-party sites.

§11 Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

  • Right of access: You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you (a Subject Access Request).
  • Right to rectification: You may ask us to correct inaccurate personal data.
  • Right to erasure: You may ask us to delete your personal data where there is no legitimate reason for us to continue processing it. Note that some data (particularly complaint audit trails) may not be erasable during their mandatory retention period.
  • Right to restriction: You may ask us to restrict processing of your personal data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to data portability: Where we process your data on the basis of your consent or a contract, you may ask us to provide it in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object: Where we process your data on the basis of legitimate interests, you may object to that processing. We will assess your objection and cease processing unless we have compelling legitimate grounds.
  • Rights relating to automated decision-making: The ADRA makes automated decisions on complaints and refund requests. You have the right to request human review of any ADRA decision. Contact us at [email protected] to exercise this right.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at [email protected]. We will respond within one calendar month of receiving your request, as required by UK GDPR.
